  • Catalysts lower activation energy by providing an alternative reaction pathway.
  • Biological catalysts (enzymes) are protein-based and highly sensitive to temperature and pH changes. They work optimally at body temperature (~37°C) and physiological pH (~7.4).
  • Non-biological catalysts are typically metals or synthetic compounds that reduce activation energy but remain sensitive to temperature changes, unlike biological catalysts they are not affected by pH.
  • Rate of reaction is inversely related to activation energy - lower activation energy means faster reaction rates.
  • Temperature effects on all reactions: Higher temperatures increase rate of reaction exponentially as more molecules possess sufficient energy to overcome the activation barrier.
  • Temperature effects on enzymes: Enzymes show optimal activity at specific temperatures; too low reduces activity, too high causes denaturation.
  • pH effects: Only affect biological catalysts - changes in pH can alter enzyme shape and active site functionality.
  • Rate comparison: The bar graph shows current rate of reaction with and without catalyst, demonstrating the catalytic advantage.
  • Rate of reaction graphs: Show how rate of reaction varies with temperature (exponential for all reactions, but enzymes also show denaturation) and pH (flat for uncatalyzed and non-biological catalysts, bell curve for enzymes).
